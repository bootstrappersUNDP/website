Leveraging Social Networks to Track Migration

This project is one of the four winners of the UNDP's Depopulation Data Challenge. The goal of the Challenge was to combine traditional and alternative data sets to better understand the process of population decline in Serbia. The challenge was organized by the UNDP Serbia Accelerator Lab in partnership with the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA).

Our team proposed two research questions. The first includes an analysis of emigration trends by collecting Facebook Ad data. The second explores the connection between migration and scientific research output of domestic scientists, using scientific publishing data through the Microsoft Academic library, a large multidisciplinary abstract and citation database of peer-reviewed literature.

Explore some of the results in the infographics, or dive deeper by investigating the destinations of Serbian emigration, or universities with Serbian academics. The most valuable contribution of this analysis is long-term: we will update the migration-stock estimates every 3-4 months which will give us the ability to see trends in emigration and estimate the number of people who leave the country each year.
